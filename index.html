THE OBSIDIAN HOUSE

Concept & Vision

Carved from the volcanic topography of the Reykjavik coastline, The Obsidian House explores the interplay between raw materiality and domestic intimacy. The structure is defined by its monolithic cast-concrete forms, punctuated by floor-to-ceiling glass that frames the shifting Arctic light.

The design prioritizes thermal mass and environmental integration, utilizing geothermal heating systems embedded within the polished basalt flooring.
